# Yellow Dragon Con — Presentation Style Guide

Inspired by the voice and brand language of [Green Dragon Fest](https://www.greendragonfest.com).
Apply this guide to all slide decks, handouts, and event communications.

---

## 1. Voice & Tone

**Enthusiastic and bold.** This is a premium fantasy experience, not a spreadsheet review.
Every slide should feel like an invitation, not a report.

**Community-first.** Attendees are a tribe, not a list. Prefer warm, belonging-focused language.

**Fantasy-infused.** Lean into the theme. Venue areas, swag tiers, and roles get evocative names.

**Direct second-person.** Write to "you," not "attendees" or "guests."

**Action-oriented.** Bullets start with verbs. No passive voice.

### Word bank — reach for these

| Instead of… | Use… |
|---|---|
| Attendees | Tribe / Fellow Raiders |
| Check-in | Join the Quest |
| Swag / Merchandise | Legendary Loot |
| Convention | Adventure / Fest |
| Levels | Dragon Raider Levels |
| Staff | Guild |

### Phrases to avoid

- "Please note that…" — cut it, just say the thing
- "As previously mentioned…" — every slide stands alone
- Passive constructions: "Dice will be distributed to…" → "Level 1 Raiders receive Dice"
- Corporate filler: "leverage," "synergy," "deliverables"

---

## 2. Slide Structure Rules

**One idea per slide.** If a slide needs two headings, it needs to be two slides.

**Max 5 bullets per content slide.** Each bullet: max 8 words. No trailing punctuation.

**No paragraphs on slides.** If copy needs more than two lines, move it to speaker notes.

**Every slide has a "so what."** The audience should be able to state the point
of each slide in one sentence. If they can't, cut or rewrite.

### Standard slide sequence for event presentations

| # | Slide type | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Title | Event name, tagline, date |
| 2 | Agenda / Quest Map | What we're covering today |
| 3–N | Content slides | One idea each |
| N+1 | Section divider | Bold single phrase, full visual |
| Last | Call to action | One clear next step |

### Section divider slides

Full-bleed background (image or color). One phrase, centered, large. No bullets.
Example: **"Roll the Dice on an Adventure of a Lifetime"**

## 3. Visual Consistency

### Color palette

| Role | Color | Hex |
|---|---|---|
| Primary / headings | Dragon Gold | `#B8860B` |
| Background (dark) | Deep Ember | `#1A1A2E` |
| Background (light alt) | Parchment | `#F5F0E8` |
| Body text on dark | Off-white | `#F0EDE6` |
| Body text on light | Charcoal | `#2C2C2C` |
| Accent / highlight | Ember Red | `#8B1A1A` |

### Typography

- **Slide titles:** Bold, 28–36pt, Dragon Gold
- **Body / bullets:** Regular, 18–22pt
- **Captions / labels:** Regular, 12–14pt, muted
- Avoid more than two font weights per slide

### Images

- Fantasy-adjacent: warm lighting, tactile textures, rich environments
- Swag images: product-forward, clean or dark background, centered
- No clip art. No stock-photo-looking people in suits.
- All images at minimum 1200px wide; stretch to fill, don't letterbox with white bars

### Slide layout grid

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  [TITLE — left-aligned, gold]           │
│  ─────────────────────────────          │
│                                         │
│  [Image or content area]                │
│                                         │
│  • Bullet one                           │
│  • Bullet two                           │
│  • Bullet three                         │
│                                         │
│  [Page number — bottom right, small]    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Left margin: consistent. Title always top-left. Page number always bottom-right.

## 4. Formatting Rules

### Capitalization

- Slide titles: Title Case
- Bullets: Sentence case (capitalize first word only)
- Level names: Always spell out in full — **Dragon Raider Level 1** (never "Lvl 1" or "L1")
- Swag items: Lowercase mid-sentence — "dice," "dice tray," "water bottle"

### Numbers

- Spell out one through nine in prose; use numerals for 10+
- Always use numerals for quantities tied to swag counts: "34 dice sets," not "thirty-four"

### Punctuation

- No periods at the end of bullets
- Em dash (—) for dramatic pauses in headers; no space around it
- Exclamation marks: maximum one per slide, use sparingly for genuine excitement

### Swag tier naming (canonical)

| Level | Full name | Swag |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Dragon Raider Level 1 | Dice |
| 2 | Dragon Raider Level 2 | Dice Tray |
| 3 | Dragon Raider Level 3 | Flagon |

Never abbreviate. Always match this table exactly.

## 5. Speaker Notes Convention

Every slide with content should have speaker notes that include:

1. **The point in one sentence** — what should the audience take away
2. **One talking point** not visible on the slide
3. **Transition cue** — how to lead into the next slide

Example:
> *Point: Level 3 Raiders are our most committed guests and get the premium swag.*
> *Talking point: Water bottles are custom-branded with the Yellow Dragon Con crest.*
> *Transition: "Which brings us to how we're distributing loot at check-in…"*
