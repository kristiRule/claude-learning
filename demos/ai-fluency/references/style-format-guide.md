## Document Structure

Every cheat sheet document follows this structure in order:

1. **Cover block** — Logo (right-aligned, top), document title (left), source attribution (italic, muted)
2. **Tips section** — One Heading 2 per tip, followed by two bullets
3. **Footer** — "AI Knowledge Bits · aiknowledgebits.com · Page X of Y"

No table of contents. No abstract. No introductory paragraph before the tips.

---

## Formatting Rules

### Headings
- Title Case for all headings
- No punctuation at the end of a heading
- Heading 2 color: Knowledge Gold (`#B8860B`)

### Bullet structure (per tip)
Each tip gets exactly two bullets — no more, no less:

1. **Quote bullet** (en-dash `–` marker): A direct verbatim quote from the source, in italics
2. **Action bullet** (triangle `▸` marker): A plain-English instruction in second person, present tense

### Body copy rules
- Second person, present tense ("Use X to do Y" — not "One should use X")
- No filler: cut "It is important to note that", "In conclusion", "As we can see"
- Max 20 words per action bullet
- No em-dashes in body copy — use a comma or a new sentence instead
- Numbers: spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10+

### Quotes
- Always verbatim — never paraphrase a quote
- Keep quotes under 25 words; trim with ellipsis if longer
- Wrap in double quotation marks: `"…"`

## Tone

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Direct and specific | Vague or hedging |
| Second person ("you", "your") | Third person ("users", "practitioners") |
| Present tense ("use", "ask") | Past or future tense |
| Concrete verbs ("prompt", "split", "score") | Nominalizations ("utilization of", "implementation of") |
| One idea per bullet | Run-on bullets cramming multiple points |
