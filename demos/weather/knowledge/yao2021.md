## Claims
- China operates the world's largest weather modification program by any measure
- Over 50,000 weather rocket launchers and thousands of aircraft sorties are conducted annually
- The program spans 23 provinces and targets drought relief, hail suppression, and fog dispersal
- China's 14th Five-Year Plan (2021–2025) calls for expanding weather modification coverage to 5.5 million km²
- Independent scientific evaluation of Chinese program efficacy is limited by data access restrictions

## Methods
Review of Chinese government policy documents, published literature, and program reports from the China Meteorological Administration. Analysis of operational statistics 1958–2020. Comparison of claimed results against available peer-reviewed evaluations.

## Findings
China has spent over $1 billion annually on weather modification in recent years. The program claims to have generated hundreds of billions of cubic meters of additional rainfall. Independent assessments cannot verify these figures due to lack of randomized controls and data access. The 2008 Beijing Olympics fog and precipitation suppression operation is the most documented single event.

## Limitations
- Most Chinese program data is not available for independent review
- No randomized controlled trials have been published for the national program
- Claimed results far exceed what peer-reviewed science would predict as achievable

## Relevance
China's program represents the outer limit of government investment in weather modification and raises questions about transboundary effects on neighboring countries including India, Kazakhstan, and Russia.

## Key Quotes
> "China's weather modification program is the largest in the world by any metric — budget, personnel, geographic coverage, or number of operations conducted annually."

> "The gap between China's claimed precipitation enhancement results and what independent scientific evaluation can verify represents one of the most significant unanswered questions in weather modification science."
