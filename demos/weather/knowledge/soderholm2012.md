## Claims
- The Queensland program used a rigorous randomized crossover design across five field seasons
- No statistically significant effect on rainfall was found at the 95% confidence level
- Hygroscopic flare seeding did produce detectable changes in cloud microphysics
- The null result illustrates the difficulty of proving cloud seeding efficacy in subtropical maritime climates
- The study is one of the most methodologically rigorous cloud seeding evaluations ever conducted

## Methods
Randomized crossover experiment over Queensland, Australia across five consecutive storm seasons. Aircraft deployed hygroscopic flares into suitable convective cloud targets. Radar-based rainfall measurement over target and control areas. Statistical analysis using paired comparison of seeded vs. non-seeded events.

## Findings
No statistically significant increase in surface rainfall was detected in seeded versus non-seeded cases. Cloud microphysics measurements confirmed seeding agents reached the target clouds. Natural variability in rainfall was too high to distinguish seeding signal from noise with the available sample size.

## Limitations
- Insufficient sample size to detect modest (10-15%) precipitation increases with statistical confidence
- Natural rainfall variability in subtropical Queensland is large, requiring many more seeded events
- Results specific to subtropical maritime convection; not directly comparable to arid or orographic settings

## Relevance
The Queensland null result is a critical counterpoint to positive findings from other programs. It demonstrates that cloud seeding efficacy is not universal and is highly dependent on cloud type, climate regime, and evaluation methodology. Essential for balanced assessment of global programs.

## Key Quotes
> "Despite clear evidence of cloud microphysical response to hygroscopic flare seeding, no statistically significant enhancement of surface rainfall was detected."

> "The natural variability of convective precipitation in Queensland is sufficiently large that detecting a 10-15% enhancement would require a sample size substantially larger than was achievable in this program."
