## Claims
- 27 U.S. states had active weather modification programs as of 2010
- The U.S. federal government has no designated lead agency for weather modification oversight
- Federal spending on weather modification research declined from a 1970s peak to near zero by 2010
- International weather modification programs, particularly China's, have grown dramatically while U.S. programs stagnated
- GAO recommends Congress designate a federal lead agency and establish a national research program

## Methods
Survey of all 50 U.S. states for active weather modification programs. Review of federal agency budgets and research portfolios. Analysis of international program trends. Stakeholder interviews with program operators, scientists, and policymakers.

## Findings
27 states operate cloud seeding programs, primarily for hail suppression and water supply augmentation. Annual U.S. investment in weather modification is approximately $15 million, compared to China's estimated $100 million per year. No federal agency has authority to coordinate or oversee the totality of U.S. weather modification activities. The scientific evidence base has not materially advanced since the 1970s due to funding cuts.

## Limitations
- State program data relies on voluntary disclosure
- International program data, especially for China, based on publicly available sources only
- Cost-benefit analyses for most programs are absent

## Relevance
Provides a concrete picture of the scale of government-sanctioned weather modification in the U.S. and globally as of 2010, establishing that these are real, funded, ongoing programs — not theoretical or fringe activities.

## Key Quotes
> "As of 2010, 27 states reported active weather modification programs, yet no federal agency has clear authority to oversee, coordinate, or evaluate the totality of these activities."

> "China's weather modification program dwarfs that of the United States in scale and investment, employing tens of thousands of operators and conducting hundreds of thousands of seeding operations annually."
