## Claims
- India's national cloud seeding experiment uses silver iodide seeding of convective clouds
- Randomized crossover design produces statistically significant rainfall increases in seeded cases
- CAIPEEX is one of the few cloud seeding programs to use rigorous randomized controlled trial design
- Results show measurable differences between seeded and control clouds in microphysical properties
- The program provides a replicable model for national-scale cloud seeding evaluation

## Methods
Randomized crossover experiment across multiple monsoon seasons over India. Aircraft-based silver iodide seeding of convective clouds. Dual-Doppler radar and aircraft in-situ measurements of cloud microphysics. Comparison of seeded vs. unseeded cloud evolution and rainfall at ground level.

## Findings
Statistically significant increases in rainfall in seeded cases compared to control. Seeded clouds show enhanced coalescence and ice-phase precipitation processes. Results support the physical basis for silver iodide cloud seeding in tropical convective systems.

## Limitations
- Results are specific to Indian monsoon convective systems; generalizability to other climates is uncertain
- Long-range downwind effects not fully characterized
- Sample size limited by availability of suitable cloud targets

## Relevance
Provides one of the strongest scientific evidence bases for cloud seeding efficacy using rigorous experimental design. Demonstrates that national government weather modification programs can be scientifically evaluated.

## Key Quotes
> "The CAIPEEX results provide statistically significant evidence for precipitation enhancement through silver iodide seeding of convective clouds in the Indian monsoon environment."

> "The randomized crossover design employed in CAIPEEX represents a methodological gold standard for evaluating cloud seeding efficacy."
